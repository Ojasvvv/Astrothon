What happened
A fragment of comet Swift-Tuttle, roughly the size of a grape, entered Earth's atmosphere at 61.2 km/s over Bavaria on 14 January 2025. It burned up completely at 21 km altitude after travelling 312 km, producing a fireball 35× brighter than a full moon. Seven camera stations across three countries captured the event — this reconstruction carries excellent scientific confidence.
